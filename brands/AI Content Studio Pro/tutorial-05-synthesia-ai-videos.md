No video editing experience required—Synthesia handles the complex work while you focus on your message and design.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Set Up Your Synthesia Account

Visit synthesia.io and click "Get started for free" or "Sign up" in the top right corner. Create your account using email, Google, or SSO options if available for your organization. Complete email verification by clicking the link sent to your inbox. Choose your plan after exploring the interface. The Free plan allows 1 video up to 1 minute for testing. The Starter plan ($22/month) includes 10 video minutes per month and 70+ avatars. The Creator plan ($67/month) offers 30 minutes monthly with all features. Enterprise plans provide unlimited videos, custom avatars, and API access. Start with a free trial to explore features before committing. After account setup, you'll land on the Synthesia dashboard showing your video library, recent creations, and templates.

### Step 2: Explore Avatar Options

Click "Create video" to begin, then explore the avatar library. Synthesia offers 140+ diverse AI avatars representing different ethnicities, ages, genders, and professional appearances. Filter avatars by diversity (ethnic backgrounds), attire (casual, business, formal), age range (young adult, middle-aged, senior), and emotion (neutral, friendly, serious). Preview avatars by clicking to see a short video sample with voice. Consider your audience and content when selecting: corporate training works well with professional business attire, marketing videos often benefit from friendly, approachable avatars, educational content suits clear, neutral presenters, and diverse content requires matching avatar to cultural context. Popular avatars include: Emma (professional female, business attire, American accent), James (friendly male, casual attire, British accent), and Sophia (corporate female, formal attire, Australian accent). Choose an avatar and click "Select" to proceed to the video editor.

### Step 3: Choose a Template or Start from Scratch

Synthesia provides templates for common use cases or blank canvas options. Template categories include corporate training (onboarding, compliance, process documentation), marketing (product demos, explainer videos, social media content), sales (pitch videos, personalized outreach, proposals), education (course content, tutorials, lectures), and internal communications (announcements, updates, reports). Browse templates by clicking "Templates" in the left sidebar and previewing designs. Templates include pre-designed layouts, placeholder text, and suggested avatar positions. To use a template, click it and select "Use template." To start from scratch, choose "Blank" for complete creative control. Blank videos offer three initial layout options: avatar only (full-screen presenter), avatar with text (split layout), and avatar with media (include images or video alongside presenter). For beginners, templates provide excellent starting structures that ensure professional results.

### Step 4: Write Your Script

Click into the script panel on the left side of the editor. Write or paste your content, keeping these principles in mind: conversational tone works better than formal written language, short sentences are easier for avatars to deliver naturally, aim for 120-150 words per minute of video, break long scripts into multiple scenes for visual variety, and write as if speaking directly to one person. Structure your script with a clear opening that hooks attention, main content organized in logical sections, smooth transitions between ideas, and a strong closing with call-to-action. Use the built-in AI script assistant if you need help. Click "AI script" and provide a topic or outline, and Synthesia generates a draft you can refine. Practical script example: "Hello, I'm Sarah from Marketing. Today, I'll show you three simple strategies to increase email open rates. First, personalize your subject lines with the recipient's name or company. Second, test send times to find when your audience is most engaged. Third, keep your message concise and scannable. Let's start with subject line personalization." This 50-word script runs approximately 25 seconds and follows clear structure.

### Step 5: Customize Avatar and Voice Settings

With your script entered, customize delivery settings. Voice selection: Click the voice dropdown to choose from 120+ voices in 60+ languages. Voices are categorized by language, accent (e.g., American English, British English), and gender. Preview voices by clicking the play icon. Match voice to avatar for realism—visual and audio should align in style. Adjust voice settings including speaking speed (0.8x for thoughtful delivery, 1.0x for standard, 1.2x for energetic), pitch (slight adjustments for personality), and emphasis (auto-detected or manual markup). Use SSML (Speech Synthesis Markup Language) for advanced control: add pauses with "... (pause) ...", emphasize words with asterisks "*important*", and control pronunciation with phonetic spelling. Position and size your avatar using the canvas controls: full screen for maximum presence, side position with content area for information-heavy videos, and picture-in-picture for demos with screen recordings.

### Step 6: Design Your Video Background and Layout

Customize visual elements to match your brand. Background options include solid colors (choose from color picker or enter brand hex codes), gradients (professional, modern appearance), uploaded images (company offices, product photos, relevant imagery), and video backgrounds (subtle motion for dynamic feel). Click "Background" in the right panel to access options. Upload your background media by clicking the upload icon or dragging files. Ensure images are high resolution (1920x1080 minimum for HD). Layout configuration: add text overlays for key points, bullet lists, or captions by clicking "Add text"; insert logos and brand elements by clicking "Add image" and uploading files; position elements using drag-and-drop or precise positioning controls; and adjust layer order so elements appear in front of or behind the avatar. Create visual hierarchy through font sizes (headings 48-72pt, body text 24-36pt), color contrast (text must be readable against backgrounds), and spacing (avoid cluttering the screen).

### Step 7: Add Scenes for Multi-Section Videos

For videos longer than 30 seconds, multiple scenes improve engagement. Click "+ Add scene" to create a new video section. Each scene can have different scripts, avatars, backgrounds, and layouts. Use scenes to break up long content into digestible segments, introduce different speakers or perspectives, show multiple examples with different visuals, and create clear chapter markers. Scene best practices include keeping scenes 15-45 seconds each for optimal pacing, using consistent design elements across scenes for cohesion, transitioning smoothly between scenes (avoid jarring changes), and considering a different avatar for scene variety if appropriate. Edit scenes by clicking the scene thumbnail in the timeline, duplicating successful scene designs with the duplicate button, reordering scenes by dragging thumbnails, and deleting unnecessary scenes with the trash icon. Multi-scene videos feel more dynamic and maintain viewer attention better than single long takes.

### Step 8: Add Media Elements

Enhance your video with additional media. Click "Media" or the "+" icon to add elements. Image insertion: upload relevant photos, graphics, or screenshots to support your message; size images appropriately and position alongside or behind the avatar; use images to visualize concepts mentioned in the script. Video clips: import screen recordings for tutorials, add product demonstration footage, or include customer testimonial clips (these play with your original audio or muted with avatar narration). Shapes and graphics: add circles, rectangles, or arrows to highlight information, create dividers between sections, or build simple diagrams. Icons: access Synthesia's icon library for common concepts, add visual interest to bullet points, or reinforce key messages. Layer management is crucial: bring elements forward or send backward using layer controls, ensure text remains readable above backgrounds, and keep avatar as focal point unless demonstrating something specific. Aim for clean designs—too many elements create visual clutter and distract from your message.

### Step 9: Preview and Refine

Before generating your final video, preview your work. Click the "Play" button to watch your video in the editor. Review carefully for script delivery and natural phrasing, timing and pacing of scenes, visual element placement and readability, avatar voice and expression appropriateness, and overall flow and engagement. Make refinements based on preview: adjust script wording if delivery sounds awkward, modify timing by adding or removing pauses, reposition visual elements that overlap or obstruct, change background if it competes with foreground elements, and adjust voice speed if too fast or slow. Use Synthesia's collaboration features if working with a team. Share draft videos for feedback using the "Share" button, leave comments on specific scenes, and implement suggestions before final generation. Take breaks during editing—fresh eyes catch issues you might miss during intensive editing sessions.

### Step 10: Generate and Download Your Video

Once satisfied with your preview, generate the final video. Click "Generate video" in the top right corner. Give your video a descriptive name for easy organization. Choose video settings including resolution (1080p for HD quality, 720p for smaller files), format (MP4 is standard and compatible with all platforms), and quality (high quality for presentations and uploads, standard for email or quick sharing). Click "Generate" to begin processing. Generation time varies based on video length: 1-minute videos take 2-5 minutes, 5-minute videos take 5-15 minutes, and longer videos process proportionally. You'll receive an email notification when generation completes. Access your video from the dashboard under "My videos." Download the video file by clicking the download icon, or use the embed code to host on your website. Share directly to YouTube, Vimeo, or social platforms using Synthesia's integration options. Export SRT subtitle files for accessibility if needed.

## Tips & Best Practices

- Keep videos under 3 minutes when possible for maximum engagement and retention
- Write scripts in short, clear sentences as if speaking to a friend or colleague
- Choose avatars that match your audience demographics and content context
- Use consistent branding across all videos (colors, fonts, logo placement)
- Add pauses in scripts for emphasis and to allow viewers to process information
- Test different voices with the same avatar to find the most natural fit
- Include text overlays for key points to reinforce spoken content
- Break longer content into multiple shorter videos rather than one long video
- Use scene changes every 30-45 seconds to maintain visual interest
- Keep backgrounds simple and non-distracting to focus attention on the message
- Preview multiple times and have others review before final generation
- Export in the highest quality your use case requires to maintain professionalism
- Consider accessibility by enabling captions and providing transcripts

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Writing for reading instead of speaking**: Scripts that look good on paper often sound robotic when spoken. Read your script aloud before finalizing to ensure natural delivery and conversational flow.
- **Choosing inappropriate avatars**: Mismatched avatars undermine credibility. A casual-dressed avatar in beachwear isn't appropriate for corporate compliance training. Match avatar formality to content seriousness.
- **Overcrowding scenes with elements**: Too many text boxes, images, and graphics compete for attention. Follow the "less is more" principle—each element should serve a clear purpose.
- **Ignoring pacing and timing**: Rushing through complex information or dragging out simple points loses viewers. Aim for natural pacing around 130-150 words per minute.
- **Using inconsistent design across scenes**: Changing fonts, colors, or styles between scenes looks unprofessional. Establish a design system and apply it consistently throughout.
- **Neglecting mobile viewing**: Many viewers watch on phones. Text that's readable on desktop may be too small on mobile. Test your video on different devices before finalizing.
- **Forgetting a call-to-action**: Videos without clear next steps waste opportunity. Always end with a specific action: visit a website, contact someone, complete training, etc.
- **Poor audio-visual alignment**: Avatar lip movements should match the voice language and speed. Choosing a voice in a different language than the script creates obvious mismatches.
- **Not utilizing scenes for long content**: Single-scene videos longer than 90 seconds become monotonous. Scene changes refresh attention and improve retention.
- **Skipping the preview step**: Generating without previewing means discovering issues only after waiting for processing. Always preview thoroughly before generating the final version.
