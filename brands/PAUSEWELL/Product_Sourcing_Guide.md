## FDA/FTC Compliance (CRITICAL)

### FDA Labeling Requirements (DSHEA)
Every supplement label MUST include:
- "Dietary supplement" designation
- Supplement Facts panel (ingredients, amounts, % Daily Value)
- Net quantity, manufacturer name/address/contact
- Directions for use and required warnings

### Allowed Claims (Structure/Function)
- "Supports hormonal balance"
- "Helps maintain mood during menopause"
- "Promotes restful sleep"
- "Supports energy and vitality"

### NOT Allowed (Disease Claims)
- "Treats menopause symptoms"
- "Cures hot flashes"
- "Prevents osteoporosis"

### Required Disclaimer
> "This statement has not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."

### FDA Notification
Must notify FDA within 30 days of first marketing with a structure/function claim.

### GMP Requirements
Manufacturer must comply with 21 CFR Part 111. All recommended manufacturers above are GMP-certified.

### Compliance Steps
1. Use GMP-certified manufacturer (all above qualify)
2. Have dietary supplement attorney review ALL label claims before printing
3. Submit FDA 30-day premarket notification
4. Use compliant disclaimer on labels, ads, website, social media
5. Maintain scientific substantiation records for every claim
6. Register facility with FDA (your 3PL handles if outsourcing)
